    • Blockchain


    Blockchain is a revolutionary technology that
    serves as a decentralized and secure digital ledger,
    recording transactions across multiple computers in
    a way that ensures data integrity and transparency.
    Unlike traditional centralized systems, blockchain operates
    on a distributed network where each transaction is grouped into br and br linked in a chronological sequence, forming a chain.


    What you will learn


    Blockchain Fundamentals

    Understanding the basic concepts of blockchain, including how it works, its history, and key terms such as distributed ledger technology, blocks, chains, nodes, and consensus mechanisms.


    Cryptography and Security

    Learning how cryptographic techniques like hashing, digital signatures, and encryption are used to ensure data integrity, security, and anonymity in blockchain networks.


    Blockchain Architecture and Development

    Gaining knowledge about different types of blockchains (public, private, consortium) and hands-on experience with blockchain development tools, programming languages like Solidity, and frameworks.


    Smart Contracts and Decentralized Applications (DApps)

    Exploring how smart contracts automate agreements and transactions on the blockchain, and how to build decentralized applications using platforms like Ethereum.


    Consensus Algorithms

    Conducting usability tests to gather user feedback, identify pain points, and make iterative improvements to the design.Understanding various consensus mechanisms such as Proof of Work (PoW), Proof of Stake (PoS), Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS), and others that maintain the integrity of the blockchain.


    Use Cases and Applications:

    Exploring real-world applications of blockchain across various industries such as finance (cryptocurrencies), supply chain, healthcare, real estate, and voting systems, along with the potential and challenges of adopting blockchain technology.
